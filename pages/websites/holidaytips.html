This tips site was a quick holiday guide for our ecommerce customers. It gave pointers, like automating common emails, but also encouraged MailChimp users to do things like "wish you customers happy holidays." I love working for people that are human, and want to encourage other folks to act human, too.

I like to mockup interactions. It's easier and quicker to talk through buildout if you have an interaction for designers and developers to point to and talk about. In this case, you can see my initial direction. I had bright colors, goofy type, and huge sliding cards.

The general interaction stayed, but Jane and Mattiel brought their perspective to the project with type and color, evening out my over-the-top tendencies. And Alan had a great idea for navigation.

He ended up building out this nav, but our Content Director, Kate Keifer, pointed out some usability issues we hadn't thought of. We went back and forth, and ended up with the final site you see at the link above.

This project is a great example of why I love how we work at MailChimp. A project is assigned, and someone kicks it off, but the end product is defined by many people. Important design decisions are made by everyone on the design team, and developers, and writers.

In the end, we have a fun, usable site with great content that works across devices. Go team.
